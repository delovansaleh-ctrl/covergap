## 3. Meta/Facebook Ad Strategy for NZ Insurance (2025–2026)

### Special Ad Category — NZ Status
- "Financial Products & Services" Special Ad Category applies in: **US, Canada, and select European countries**
- **NZ is NOT in the mandatory zone** — CoverGap does NOT need to declare Special Ad Category
- This means: **age targeting (e.g., 35–55) IS still available in NZ**
- However: Interest *exclusions* were removed by Meta in March 2025
- Interest categories were consolidated/broadened in June 2025

### Platform Benchmarks (Finance/Insurance, 2025)
- Average CPC: **$3.77–4.57** (highest of all industries — very competitive)
- Lead gen campaign CTR: **2.53%** (vs 1.57% for traffic campaigns)
- Lead gen CPC: **$1.88**
- NZ CPM/CPC: ~24% below global average (cheaper than US/UK)
- AI-driven ads (Advantage+): 22% higher returns in 2024

### What Targeting Works for NZ Insurance Leads

**Demographics:**
- Age: **30–55** (sweet spot — mortgage age, young family, income to protect)
- Location: New Zealand (all regions — don't over-restrict)
- Gender: All (don't exclude — both partners make insurance decisions)

**Manual Interests that still work:**
- Life insurance
- Health insurance
- Income protection
- Financial planning
- Personal finance
- Investment
- Mortgage / home loan
- KiwiSaver
- First home buyers (NZ-specific)

**Behaviours:**
- Homeowners (if available)
- Parents (if available under NZ targeting)

**Custom/Lookalike (if you have pixel data):**
- Lookalike from website visitors
- Lookalike from Instagram/Facebook page engagers
- Retargeting people who visited covergap.nz but didn't submit

### Advantage+ vs Manual — The Reality in 2026

**Meta's direction:** Pushing hard toward Advantage+. AI ads show 22% better results.

**The tradeoff:**
- Manual: You control who sees your ads. Better for testing creative. Lower CPM initially but may stall.
- Advantage+: Meta's AI finds your audience. Higher CPM/CPC upfront but self-optimises. Works better once it has ~50+ conversions to learn from.

**Recommendation for CoverGap at $25/day budget:**
- Start with MANUAL targeting (as Delovan wants) — small budget needs focused audience
- After 50+ leads collected, build Advantage+ Lookalike from lead list
- Creative quality matters MORE than audience precision at this stage

## 4. Ad Creative — What Actually Converts

### Video Hook (first 2–3 seconds — make or break)
Best performing hooks for insurance:
- **Problem/fear:** "Most Kiwi families don't realise how much they're overpaying for life insurance"
- **Surprising stat:** "Your bank charges up to 40% more for the same life cover"
- **Direct question:** "When did you last check if your insurance cover still makes sense?"
- **Empathy:** "We know — insurance feels complicated. That's why we do the hard part for you."

### Video Format Best Practices
- Length: **Under 45 seconds** (30 seconds is ideal)
- Caption everything — 85% watch without sound
- Show a real face in first 3 seconds (face = trust)
- Include 2–3 qualifying questions in ad copy ("Do you have a mortgage? Dependants under 18?")
- End with specific CTA: "Free 20-minute review. No obligation."
- Works at 9:16 (Reels) and 1:1 (Feed)

### Ad Types by Effectiveness (Insurance)
1. Talking-head video (real adviser or testimonial) — highest trust
2. Short explainer animation — good for cold audiences
3. Carousel (before/after cost comparison) — works for retargeting
4. Static image with bold hook text — cheapest to test

### Copy Formula That Works
```
Hook (scroll stopper): [Pain point or surprising fact]
Problem: [What most people are doing wrong]
Solution: [How CoverGap fixes it — independent review]
Social proof: [Brief credibility signal]
CTA: Free cover review. 20 minutes. No obligation.
```

## 5. Lead Qualification — How the Pros Do It

Growth Academy's 12+ data point form includes:
- Full name
- Email
- Phone number
- Type of cover interested in
- Whether they currently have cover
- Approximate annual income (determines premium range)
- Whether they have a mortgage
- Number of dependants
- Best time to call

**CoverGap Instant Form should ideally ask:**
1. Name + phone (minimum — already have)
2. Do you currently have life/health insurance? (Yes/No)
3. Do you have a mortgage or dependants? (Yes/No)
4. Best time for a free call?

More qualification = lower volume but higher conversion rate.
